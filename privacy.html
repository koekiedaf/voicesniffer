VoiceSniffer — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 June 2026

VoiceSniffer is built to be private by default. Here's exactly what happens to your data.

On-device by default

All transcription in Chat and Review mode runs entirely on your Mac using on-device speech-recognition models (WhisperKit / Parakeet). Your audio and transcripts never leave your device in these modes. There is no account, no sign-in, and no analytics or tracking of any kind.

Microphone

VoiceSniffer uses the microphone only to record audio that you choose to transcribe. Recordings are processed locally and stored only on your Mac.

Screen & System Audio Recording

If you enable "system audio" (for example to capture the other side of a video call), VoiceSniffer uses macOS Screen & System Audio Recording solely to capture that audio for transcription. It is processed locally; nothing is sent anywhere.

Cloud transcription (optional — Meeting mode only)

Meeting mode offers an optional online engine powered by Gladia (gladia.io). You only use it if you enter your own Gladia API key and select the cloud option. In that case, the recorded audio is uploaded to Gladia to produce the transcript. This is the only situation in which any audio leaves your device. If you don't provide a Gladia key, Meeting mode runs fully offline. See Gladia's own privacy policy at https://www.gladia.io for how they process that data.

Where your data is stored

Recordings, transcripts and settings are stored locally in ~/Library/Application Support/VoiceSniffer. Your Gladia API key (if entered) is stored in the macOS Keychain. You can remove everything at any time via Settings → Uninstall.

Data collection

The developer collects no personal data, runs no analytics, and does not track you.
